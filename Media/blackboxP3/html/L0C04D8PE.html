Yemeni Government Forces Entered Aden

  •      The Yemeni army has arrived at the airport in Aden.
  •    The two sides in the conflict in Aden are both suppose to be fighting the Houthi Shiites backed by the Islamic Republic of Iran.
  •    Saudi Arabia and the UAE see Iran as their common enemy, but in the southern part of Yemen, both countries have different interests.

    •     Government forces have now managed to re-enter the strategic port three weeks after leaving Aden.According to the latest news from Yemen, Aden Airport is under the control of government forces.The government forces succeeded in seizing the airport and other areas of the Aden port from separatist forces in southern Yemen, German news agencies reported Wednesday quoting Yemen’s security circles.Yemeni separatists in the southern part of Yemen have surrendered after fierce clashes.

        Prior to that, army forces occupied the center of Zinjibar province after fierce fighting with the separatists in the south of Yemen.For nearly two weeks, the port of Aden and its surrounding areas have been the scene of violent clashes between President Abdul Mansour Hadi‘s supportive forces and militants calling themselves the Transitional Council of Yemen.

       The two sides in the conflict in Aden are both suppose to be fighting the Houthi Shiites backed by the Islamic Republic of Iran, and both enjoy the support of the Saudi-led coalition.After clashes between separatists in the south and forces loyal to the government, Abdar Mansour Hadi and most of his cabinet ministers settled in Riyadh.Yemeni separatists in southern Yemen were then able to seize the presidential palace in the port of Aden and most of the important government buildings.

       The Transitional Council of Southern Yemen called for the division of Yemen into the southern and northern parts.Prior to the unification of Yemen, the country was divided into north and south.

       Split in the Saudi Arabian Coalition

        Clashes between forces loyal to Abdur Mansur Hadi and separatists in southern Yemen showed a deep rift in the Arab coalition.The UAE, which is a member of the Arab coalition, supports the Transitional Council of the South and Saudi Arabia supports the legal government of Abdul Mansour Hadi.

       The emergence of this rift and the continuing clashes weakened Riyadh’s position in Yemen against the Iranian-backed Houthis.The United Arab Emirates and Saudi Arabia had a common stance against Iran, especially in a coalition against Iran-backed Houthis.Saudi Arabia and the UAE see Iran as their common enemy, but in the southern part of Yemen, both countries have different interests.

       In Yemen, the devastating proxy war between pro-Iranian Shiite rebels and Yemeni government forces has been ongoing for years.Government forces are backed by the Saudi-led Arab coalition.The war in Yemen began when the Houthis occupied the northern part of the country, including its capital, Sana’a, and ousted the constitutional government recognized by the United Nations.

       The United Nations has long sought to persuade all parties involved in the Yemeni crisis to find a peaceful solution to ending the crisis in the region.The Yemeni civil war has starved millions of its citizens, and according to the United Nations, the crisis has become the world’s largest humanitarian disaster.
