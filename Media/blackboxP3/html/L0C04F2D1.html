Evidence for routine brain tumor imaging is murky, but research can shed light

     After treatment, medical staff use routine brain scans to monitor progress in brain tumor patients.However, how often such scans should occur is unclear.A Position Statement from a large group of UK experts highlights that the evidence behind current scanning routines is weak.The article highlights numerous ways that researchers can gather evidence to improve scanning routines so that they are most effective and do not interfere with patient quality of life.
