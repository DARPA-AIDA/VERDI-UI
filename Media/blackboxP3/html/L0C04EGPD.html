US Targets al-Qaeda Headquarters in Idlib Missile Attacks

  •    Earlier reports from Idlib indicated that the area had been quiet for hours on Saturday after Russian-backed Syrian forces unilaterally launched a ceasefire.
  •    The US and Turkey have serious disagreements over the future of the region in northeast Syria.

    •     The US Army Command says it has targeted al-Qaeda’s headquarters in Syria for rocket attacks.The attack took place in an area of ​​Idlib province that is still controlled by opposition and rebels against the government of Bashar al-Assad.Russia, meanwhile, has announced the start of a ceasefire in Idlib on Saturday.

        The US military’s headquarters said the missile attack targeted those 'responsible for attacking and threatening American civilians, their allies, and killing innocent civilians.'

       The Pentagon has not released further details of the attack, but other reports have claimed the deaths of three people during the missile attack.The attack comes minutes after Syrian and Russian government forces announced the start of a ceasefire in Idlib on Saturday, August 31st.

       Earlier reports from Idlib indicated that the area had been quiet for hours on Saturday after Russian-backed Syrian forces unilaterally launched a ceasefire.

       Threatening Turkey

       In another development, Turkey has warned that it is preparing an operation aimed at creating a 'safe zone' in northeast Syria.Turkey, however, said the operation would only begin if talks between Ankara and Washington failed on the Kurdish issue and Syria’s northeast.

       'In the next few weeks, if our troops cannot gain real control in the area, then we will have no choice but to carry out our operations,' Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan said Saturday.

       The US and Turkey have serious disagreements over the future of the region in northeast Syria.While the US backs the Kurdish Peshmerga in the region, and joined them in their battle with ISIS, Turkey is threatening the Kurds’ power in the north and calls for Kurdish forces to withdraw from the area.

       Turkey calls the People’s Defense Units a terrorist group, saying it is affiliated with PKK‘s arm.

        Start of a ceasefire in Idlib

       Meanwhile, Russia announced Friday morning that forces loyal to Bashar al Assad’s government will begin a ceasefire in Idlib.The unilateral ceasefire started at 9 am on Saturday, the Russian Defense Ministry said.

       The United Nations says hundreds of thousands have fled from Idlib.Nearly 2 million people live in Idlib.Previous efforts to stop the conflict in the region have failed.A day before the ceasefire was announced, United Nations Emergency Relief Coordinator Mark Lukoc urged the UN Security Council to take meaningful action to support Idlib’s civilians.

       Hundreds of thousands have fled the area since last year, and several towns and villages have been merged.Syrian change watchdogs say at least 3,000 people fled Idlib city on Friday alone.

       Reports of clashes on parts of the Syrian-Turkish border have been released on Friday as civilians flee.

       Idlib is the latest stronghold of rebels and jihadist groups in northwest Syria who have been fighting for the fall of Assad since the fall of 2008.The province of Idlib is in control of several rival groups opposed to Assad.
