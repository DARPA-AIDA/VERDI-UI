U.S. Helps Russia Thwart St.Petersburg Bombing

  •      Russia arrested on Monday two men suspected of planning the New Year’s Eve attack.
  •    A number of The local news sites reported that the suspects were at an advanced stage of planning attacks on a shopping mall and cathedral.
  •    Elsewhere, the US President, Donald Trump announced that he will Sign a Partial Trade Agreement with China on January 15.

    •     US President Donald Trump said on Tuesday that 'great and important coordination' between the United States and Russia helped prevent an attack on St.Petersburg.'President Putin of Russia called to thank me and the U.S. for informing them of a planned terrorist attack in the very beautiful city of Saint Petersburg,' Trump wrote on his Twitter account.'They were able to quickly apprehend the suspects, with many lives being saved . ..Great and important coordination!'The US president added.

        Russia arrested on Monday two men suspected of planning the New Year’s Eve attack.According to the Russian Federal security service, FSB, the two men had pledged allegiance to the Islamic State (IS) jihadist group.The FSB said the two men were arrested Friday based on information provided by 'American partners,' and that they both confessed to having been planning attacks.

       They were identified as Nikita Semyonov, 22, and Georgiy Chernyshov, 23.A number of The local news sites reported that the suspects were at an advanced stage of planning attacks on a shopping mall and cathedral and sent photos to IS contacts to confirm the targets.

       Elsewhere, the US President, Donald Trump announced that he will Sign a Partial Trade Agreement with China on January 15, 2020.President Trump made the announcement on Tuesday that he will sign the partial agreement after months of trade war.'I will be signing our very large and comprehensive Phase One Trade Deal with China on January 15,' Trump tweeted.

        He added that senior Beijing officials will be present to witness the signing.'The ceremony will take place at the White House.High-level representatives of China will be present,' said President Trump.He also added that 'at a later date I will be going to Beijing where talks will begin on Phase Two!'President Trump did not add any further details.

       China’s top trade negotiator, Vice Premier Liu He, is also widely expected to lead a delegation to Washington for the first phase signing ceremony.Since March 2018, Beijing and Washington have imposed reciprocal tariffs of hundreds of billions of dollars on their goods, which is seriously affecting China in a negative way and slowing the world economy.

       According to Washington, the deal includes advances in technology transfer, as well as better access to the Chinese market for financial companies.It also says Beijing will buy $200 billion in US products for a two-year period from a 2017 benchmark.In exchange for the Chinese commitments made in mid-December, the Trump administration gave up imposing new tariffs.Under the terms of the deal, it also agreed that it would cut by half the tariff rate it imposed on Sept 1 on a $120 billion list of Chinese goods, to 7.5%.
