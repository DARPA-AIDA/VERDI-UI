U.S. Election Enters Final Sprint, Early Voters All Time High

  •    There is not much difference between the two parties’ early voting in Florida; but in Pennsylvania, Democratic voters are three times as many as Republican voters.
  •    Texas also released data, showing that the number of early voters in the state exceeded 9 million.

    •     The unabated momentum of the epidemic and the weak economic recovery, the United States will usher in the end of the election in three days and give a vote of confidence in Trump’s four years in power.Statistics show that Americans’ enthusiasm for participation is unprecedentedly high.

        At present, more than 90 million people, accounting for more than a quarter of the total population, have voted in advance, breaking the historical record of American elections.

       This broke the record for the number of early votes in the U.S. general election over the years, and was nearly double the 2016 figure (47 million people).

       The number of people who voted early this year also accounted for 65% of the total number of votes in 2016 (about 138 million people).

       Reuters believes that this record-breaking figure reflects the unprecedented enthusiasm of American voters.The New York Times described the raging epidemic, delays in mailing ballots, long queues waiting to vote, and court rulings on election results, all testing confidence in the US electoral system.

       In North Carolina, Florida, Georgia, and Arizona, the number of early voters exceeded 80% of the total number of people who participated in the vote 4 years ago, and Wisconsin and Michigan doubled the number 4 years ago.The New York Times believes that this year may be the first time in the history of the US election that more than 150 million people voted.

       What deserves special attention is the situation in the key swing states.As of the 31st, Florida has 8.29 million voters early voting, accounting for 59% of the total number of voters.

       In Pennsylvania, 2.37 million mailed ballots have been submitted (the number of early votes at the station is not counted), of which 1.57 million are registered Democrats and 540,000 are registered Republicans.The gap between the two parties is obvious.

       In terms of age, elderly voters over the age of 66 have the highest voting enthusiasm, with a total of 920,000 early voting, accounting for 38.9% of the total.

       There is not much difference between the two parties’ early voting in Florida; but in Pennsylvania, Democratic voters are three times as many as Republican voters.

       On Friday, Texas also released data, showing that the number of early voters in the state exceeded 9 million, which has exceeded the total number of votes in the state of Texas in the last general election.

       In summary, in the 20 states where voters’ party identities were announced, there were approximately 19.9 million registered Democrats in the early voting, 6.9 million more than 13 million registered Republicans.But party status is not necessarily equated with their voting results.

       The Associated Press stated that it cannot be based on the results of the early voting to draw the Democratic lead.Because polls show that many Republican voters believe Trump’s warning about mailing votes, they plan to wait until November 3 to vote in person.

       Therefore, the early voting fever of the Democratic Party may be slowly caught up by the Republican Party.

        Although Trump continues to lag behind in the polls, the 'Capitol Hill' still pointed out a 'turnover' plan for the US president: Trump must win Florida and Pennsylvania.As long as he can keep Arizona and other states that he won four years ago, even if he loses Michigan and Wisconsin, he can still be re-elected.

       According to the RCP poll website, Biden currently leads Trump by an average of 1.2% in Florida and 3.6% in Pennsylvania.The elections in the two places are still stalemate.And Biden’s popularity among Latinos in Florida has also aroused the concerns of local Democrats.

       The countdown to polling day in the U.S. general election, President Trump and the Democratic presidential candidate Biden, who are fighting for re-election, stepped up the campaign in the key swing states of Pennsylvania and Michigan in the last weekend before the election.

       Trump held four campaign rallies in Pennsylvania on Saturday.Trump told his supporters that 'the country will realize the American dream.'Trump stated that the United States is only a few weeks away from mass distribution of the new coronavirus vaccine.
