    •     The United States Supreme Court rejected an unprecedented attempt By Texas to overturn the results of the 2020 US presidential election in four swing states, Georgia, Michigan, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsinan in an effort that US President Donald Trump supported.

        Joe Biden got more votes in the four states miraculously after Trump was ahead by tens to hundreds of thousands of votes in each state until the early morning following the election.Nineteen attorneys general (Department of Justice) and 106 members of the Texas Congress supported the lawsuit filed with the Supreme Court to annul the election result.

       But the court rejected this lawsuit Friday, based on the fact that the legal position of the state of Texas does not allow it to file a lawsuit, which came in the form of a decision issued by the court to reject the request of this US state.

       The court’s decision stated: 'Texas has not demonstrated a judicially cognizable interest in the manner in which another state conducts its elections.'

       The Supreme Court’s rejection of this request adds a new failure for Trump, who had previously suggested that the results of last November’s elections would be settled in the Supreme Court.Earlier this week, the Supreme Court rejected a lawsuit challenging Democratic candidate Biden’s victory in Pennsylvania, a rejection stated in a one-sentence ruling.
